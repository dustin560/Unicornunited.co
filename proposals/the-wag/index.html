01 — The Opportunity

Don't restore. Reposition.

Chris Sullivan and The WAG represent something rare: a founder and a venue that genuinely shaped British culture — but whose story has been quietly overtaken by time, gentrification, and the erasure of Soho's creative identity.

There's no scandal. No reputational crisis. What's happened is subtler: the WAG and Sullivan have been forgotten by mainstream culture while remaining revered within their niche. The WAG's values — cross-cultural, genre-fluid, democratically elitist, creatively rigorous — are more aligned with today's cultural conversation than they were in the 1990s.

The opportunity: position the WAG not as an 80s throwback, but as the original blueprint for what good nightlife culture looks like — and build something entirely new on that foundation.

33 Wardour Street — where Afrika Bambaataa played the UK's first hip-hop event — is now an O'Neill's pub. That single image captures everything this project is about.

04 — Social Enterprise

A Community Interest Company.

The WAG Creative Haus is a natural fit for a Community Interest Company (CIC) — a UK social enterprise structure that combines commercial trading with a legally protected social mission. CIC registrations are growing 16% year-on-year, with arts, education, and culture the most common sectors.

Chris keeps control

Unlike a charity, CIC founders can be directors, draw a salary, and retain creative control. Dividends capped at 35% of distributable profits. Sullivan stays at the helm.

Unlocks grant funding

Access to Arts Council, National Lottery, DCMS Grassroots Music Fund, social investment, and CSR partnerships a standard limited company can't access.

Mission is protected

The "asset lock" ensures The WAG's cultural empowerment mission can't be stripped out and sold. Purpose is structurally preserved.

Purpose attracts partners

"Social enterprise" adds a purpose narrative brands increasingly want to associate with. The CIC structure is a differentiator when The Studio pitches.

Can trade commercially

A CIC is not a charity. It can sell tickets, charge agency fees, do brand partnerships. Activities just have to benefit the community.

Proven in culture

Edinburgh's Bongo Club has run as a social enterprise nightclub since 1996 — 30 years and counting. The model works.

Nightclub → record label → 50,000 sq ft lifestyle hub. Lesson: club as philosophy that extends into other formats.
